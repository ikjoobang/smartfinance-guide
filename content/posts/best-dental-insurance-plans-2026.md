**‚úÖ Factor 1: Coverage Limits and What is Actually Covered**

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q1. How much does dental insurance typically cost in 2026?**
Dental insurance costs range from $29 to $52 per month for standard coverage in 2026. Your actual rate depends on factors including your location, age, credit score, and coverage amount.

**Q2. Is dental insurance tax deductible?**
In most cases, personal dental insurance premiums are not tax deductible. However, if you use it for business purposes, you may be able to deduct the premiums as a business expense. Consult a tax professional for your specific situation.

**Q3. Can I cancel my dental insurance policy at any time?**
Most dental insurance policies can be cancelled at any time with no penalty. Some providers offer prorated refunds for unused premium. Always check the cancellation terms before signing up.

**Q4. What is not covered by dental insurance?**
Common exclusions include intentional damage, wear and tear, pre-existing conditions (for health-related policies), and acts of war. Each provider has different exclusions, so review the policy carefully.

**Q5. How long does it take to get dental insurance coverage?**
Most dental insurance providers can issue a policy within 24-48 hours. Some offer instant approval for standard applications. More complex applications may take 1-2 weeks for underwriting review.
