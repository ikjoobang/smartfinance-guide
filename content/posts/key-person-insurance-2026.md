## What Is Key Person Insurance and Why Does Your Business Need It?

Key person insurance is a life insurance policy that a business purchases on the life of a critical employee, executive, or owner. The business pays the premiums, owns the policy, and is the beneficiary. If the key person dies or becomes permanently disabled, the business receives the death benefit to cover financial losses during the transition period.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q1. Is key person insurance tax-deductible for the business?**
No. Under IRS rules (IRC Section 264), premiums paid for key person insurance are not tax-deductible because the business is the beneficiary. However, the death benefit is received tax-free under IRC Section 101, making it a net tax advantage.

**Q2. Can the key person also be covered by their own personal life insurance?**
Yes. Key person insurance and personal life insurance are completely separate. The business-owned policy has no impact on the individual's personal coverage or insurability.

**Q3. What happens to the key person insurance if the employee leaves?**
The business has several options: cancel the policy and stop premium payments, transfer ownership to the departing employee (often as part of a separation package), or maintain the policy if the business still has an insurable interest.

**Q4. Do lenders require key person insurance?**
Many SBA lenders and venture capital investors require key person insurance as a condition of funding. Typical requirements range from 1â€“2x the loan amount or investment. This protects their financial interest in the business.

**Q5. How is key person insurance different from buy-sell insurance?**
Key person insurance benefits the business entity to cover financial losses. Buy-sell insurance funds the purchase of a deceased owner's share by surviving partners or the business. They serve different purposes and are often purchased together.
