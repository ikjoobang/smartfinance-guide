## What Coverage Do You Actually Need?

**Most states require minimum liability coverage, but that's rarely enough to protect you financially.** Here's what each coverage type does and who needs it.

&nbsp;

| Coverage Type | What It Covers | Required? | Recommended Minimum |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bodily Injury Liability | Others' medical bills in accidents you cause | Yes (most states) | $100,000/$300,000 |
| Property Damage Liability | Others' property you damage | Yes (most states) | $100,000 |
| Collision | Your car in accidents | No (unless financed) | Actual cash value |
| Comprehensive | Theft, weather, animals | No (unless financed) | Actual cash value |
| Uninsured Motorist | Accidents with uninsured drivers | Varies by state | Match your liability limits |
| Medical Payments | Your medical bills | No | $5,000-$10,000 |

&nbsp;

The Insurance Information Institute recommends 100/300/100 liability limits as a baseline for most drivers. If you have significant assets, consider an umbrella policy for additional protection.

&nbsp;

If you're financing or leasing your car, your lender typically requires both collision and comprehensive coverage. Once you own your car outright, dropping these can save $500-$1,000 annually — but only if you can afford to replace the car out of pocket.

## How to Save on Auto Insurance in 2026

**The single most effective way to lower your auto insurance rate is to compare quotes from at least 3-5 companies.** Beyond that, these strategies consistently save the most money.

&nbsp;

■ Top Savings Strategies (ranked by average savings)

&nbsp;

❶ **Bundle home + auto** — Average savings: 15-25% (National Association of Insurance Commissioners data)

❷ **Increase your deductible** — Raising from $500 to $1,000 saves 15-20% on collision/comprehensive

❸ **Ask about all available discounts** — Good driver, good student, low mileage, paperless billing

❹ **Maintain good credit** — Drivers with excellent credit pay 40-60% less in most states (not available in CA, HI, MA)

❺ **Consider usage-based insurance** — Programs like Progressive Snapshot or Allstate Drivewise can save up to 30%

&nbsp;

One often-overlooked strategy: if you drive under 7,500 miles per year, ask about low-mileage discounts. Several insurers offer 5-15% off for low-mileage drivers.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

&nbsp;

**Q. How much auto insurance do I need?**

Most financial experts recommend at least 100/300/100 liability coverage. If you have assets over $300,000, consider an umbrella policy. The minimum state requirements are often too low to protect you in a serious accident.

&nbsp;

**Q. Is the cheapest auto insurance always the best?**

Not necessarily. A low premium means nothing if the company denies legitimate claims. Check J.D. Power claims satisfaction scores and AM Best financial strength ratings before choosing based on price alone.

&nbsp;

**Q. How often should I shop for new auto insurance quotes?**

At least once a year, or whenever you experience a life change (moving, buying a car, marriage, turning 25). Insurance companies change their pricing algorithms frequently.

&nbsp;

**Q. Does my credit score affect my auto insurance rate?**

Yes, in most states. Drivers with poor credit pay 40-100% more than those with excellent credit, according to the Insurance Information Institute. California, Hawaii, and Massachusetts prohibit credit-based insurance scoring.

&nbsp;

**Q. Can I switch auto insurance companies mid-policy?**

Yes, you can switch at any time. Most companies will refund the unused portion of your premium. Just make sure your new policy starts before you cancel the old one to avoid a coverage gap.

&nbsp;

**Q. What's the difference between collision and comprehensive coverage?**

Collision covers damage from accidents with other vehicles or objects. Comprehensive covers non-collision events like theft, vandalism, weather damage, and animal strikes. Both are optional unless your car is financed.

&nbsp;

**Q. Do I need gap insurance?**

If you owe more on your car loan than the car is worth, gap insurance covers the difference if your car is totaled. It's highly recommended for new cars and long-term financing.
